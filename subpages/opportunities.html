Alta Vista Ilocandia

Previous Next

Alta Vista Ilocandia is located at Brgy. Caparispisan on a 4-hectare lush rolling mountain environment on Pagudpud's northern outskirts, with a 180-degree view of the West Philippine Sea and Cordillera Mountain Ranges. The recently opened resort is bordered by pristine reefs, a scenic mountain range, and the soothing beauty of the West Philippine Sea's turquoise-blue waves, which are interwoven with powder-like white sand beach bays.


Bantay Abot Cave

Previous Next

The Bantay Abot caves are found in Balaoi's Sitio Gaoa. It refers to a sea mountain having a gap in it. Waves gradually eroded the naturally formed hole until it was destroyed by an earthquake in the 1980s. It looks like a gigantic donut! The waves continue to wash through the hole, sculpting it. Because the cave's top is covered in lush green foliage, it's also known as the underground sea.


Blue Lagoon

Previous Next

Malingay Cove in Sitio Malingay is home to the Blue Lagoon, or Maira-ira as it is often called. This hidden bay is located at Mairaira Point and is shaped like a half moon, with fine white sands that contrast sharply with the turquoise, or cerulean blue, seas.


Don Hermanos Islands

Previous Next

Barangay Balaoi is home to Dos Hermanos Island. The islands are made up of two mountains that are nearly identical. According to legend, there were two brothers who were very close to each other many years ago. They had made a promise to each other that they would stay together till death.


Hannah's Beach Resort

Previous Next

Hannah's Beach Resort and Convention Center, located in Pagudpud, Ilocos Norte, Philippines, is a seven-hectare resort. It is located on the famous Blue Lagoon's shoreline, a stunning stretch of immaculate white sand beach that is partially encircled by a range of mountains and cliffs that embrace the area, producing a natural sanctuary of beauty and charm.


Kabigan Falls

Previous Next

Kabigan Falls is a must-see for everyone visiting the area. The falls, which are located in Barangay Balaoi, are a renowned tourist attraction in Pagudpud (close to Bantay Abot). When there is a lot of water running, the cool waters cascade 87 feet into a natural concave basin, which is quite impressive. The name comes from the Filipino words "kaibigan" and "magkaibigan."
